# Ramp AI Adoption Playbook (Context for Glass)

How Ramp got to **99.5% team AI adoption + 6,300% YoY AI usage growth** in ~15 months. Published April 8 2026 by **Geoff Charles** (Ramp CPO). Glass is the technical mechanism; this playbook is the cultural/organizational one.

**Why this matters for Tinker**: Tinker enables the same playbook for other orgs. Without the playbook, the tool alone won't drive adoption. Keep this context in mind when building features — they need to serve this kind of org-wide rollout.

## Headline Numbers

- `[2026-04-09]` **AI usage up 6,300% from last year**
- `[2026-04-09]` **99.5% of the team active on AI tools**
- `[2026-04-09]` **84% using coding agents weekly**
- `[2026-04-09]` **1,500+ apps shipped** on internal platform in **6 weeks** from **800+ builders**
- `[2026-04-09]` **12% of all production PRs from non-engineers** via Ramp Inspect coding agent
- `[2026-04-09]` Largest AI hackathon: **700 participants** across sellers, CX, legal, marketing, finance

## The Starting Condition

- `[2026-04-09]` Jan 2025 kickoff: leadership told the whole company they would become "the most productive company in the world"
- `[2026-04-09]` Started with: leadership clarity that AI usage is expectation, dedicated AI "guild", Slack channels for sharing builds, all-hands to celebrate builders, mandated usage + tracking
- `[2026-04-09]` **No formal change management program. No mandatory training curriculum.** Built infrastructure for self-teaching.

## AI Proficiency Ladder (L0 → L3)

- `[2026-04-09]` **L0** — Sometimes uses ChatGPT. No workflow changes. ("If you're still here and not self-starting, you will most likely not be at the company.")
- `[2026-04-09]` **L1** — Built custom GPTs, used Notion agents, dabbled in Claude Code. Starting to see what's possible.
- `[2026-04-09]` **L2** — Built an app that automates part of their job. Committed code or contributed feedback. "Where things get real."
- `[2026-04-09]` **L3** — Systems builders. Don't just use AI — build infrastructure that levels everyone else up. "Force multipliers."

**Relevance to Tinker**: the product should meet L0–L1 where they are (the "raise the floor" thesis — see [[ramp-glass]]) while preserving L2–L3 power (don't limit the ceiling).

## Org Design: Build From Center, Drive From Spokes

- `[2026-04-09]` Initial failure #1: centralized one small team to build tools for everyone — demand outstripped capacity
- `[2026-04-09]` Initial failure #2: decentralized — every team builds their own — redundant re-learning
- `[2026-04-09]` **Answer: do both.** Small central team builds platforms/connectors/plumbing. Functional teams build on top and feed back priorities.

## Leaderboard + Competition

- `[2026-04-09]` Internal leaderboard tracking AI usage across every team and individual — sessions run, skills used, apps shipped, tools connected. **Visible to everyone.**
- `[2026-04-09]` Three dynamics from the leaderboard:
  - Healthy peer pressure — nobody wants bottom
  - Manager accountability — team-level rankings impossible to ignore
  - Discovery through emulation — leaderboard doubles as a map of what top performers use

## Remove Every Constraint

- `[2026-04-09]` **Kill token limits + access restrictions.** No caps, no tiered access by role.
- `[2026-04-09]` **Treat AI usage as infinite learning budget.** Don't demand ROI on every token before people have learned.
- `[2026-04-09]` **Remove every IT bottleneck on connectors.** Pre-connected 30+ tools so one SSO auth unlocks everything.
- `[2026-04-09]` **CFO reframe**: "If someone is 2x more productive with AI, you should be willing to spend their entire salary again in tokens."

## Creative Destruction

- `[2026-04-09]` "Many tools we shipped in January 2026 are already obsolete — replaced by better versions, often from the same builders. Shelf life of weeks, not months."
- `[2026-04-09]` "If your internal tools from three months ago still feel state-of-the-art, you're not moving boldly enough."
- `[2026-04-09]` "People aren't attached to their tools. They're attached to their problems."

## "Aha Moment" Philosophy

- `[2026-04-09]` "The single biggest unlock is getting someone to experience a real result on day one."
- `[2026-04-09]` Early converts mattered more than anything — one ambitious sales ops lead, one frustrated product operator per team becomes the contagion

**Relevance to Tinker**: first-run flow is critical. FirstRun must produce a useful result, not a setup wizard.

## Hiring & Performance Signal

- `[2026-04-09]` **Absolute requirement for anyone joining Ramp: proficient with AI tools. No exceptions.**
- `[2026-04-09]` PM candidates have a dedicated interview session: build a product, show how, walk through
- `[2026-04-09]` Performance reviews evaluate AI proficiency as a core skill
- `[2026-04-10]` Ramp hiring **"AI Product Operator"** role — deploy workflows for non-engineering teams, drive >90% active usage, own QA/dashboards, coach internal champions
